# ashganda.com Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Tagline**: Enterprise Technology Strategy & Insights
**Position**: Premier thought leadership for enterprise technology executives
**Author**: Ash Ganda

## Target Audience

### Primary
- **CTOs** at mid-to-large enterprises
- **Technology executives** making platform decisions
- **Enterprise architects** evaluating solutions
- **Digital transformation leaders**

### Secondary
- Tech investors and analysts
- Senior engineering managers
- Management consultants

### Audience Characteristics
- 10+ years experience in technology leadership
- Managing budgets >$1M annually
- Focused on strategic outcomes, not just technical details
- Time-poor, value density over length
- Skeptical of vendor hype, want objective analysis

## Voice & Tone

### Core Tone
- **Authoritative**: Speak from deep expertise and experience
- **Strategic**: Focus on business outcomes, not just technology
- **Forward-looking**: Anticipate trends and their implications
- **Balanced**: Present multiple perspectives, acknowledge tradeoffs
- **Concise**: Dense insights, no filler content

### Writing Style
- Use executive-friendly language
- Lead with business impact, support with technical details
- Reference industry analysts (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Include competitive positioning insights
- Avoid vendor marketing language

### Vocabulary
**Use**: strategic, platform, architecture, transformation, governance, scale, enterprise, modernization, competitive advantage, ROI
**Avoid**: simple, easy, just, basic, game-changer, revolutionary, silver bullet

## Content Structure

### Ideal Article Structure
1. **Executive Hook** (100-150 words): Why this matters NOW for enterprise leaders
2. **Strategic Context** (200-300 words): Market forces, competitive dynamics
3. **Analysis Section 1** (300-400 words): Deep dive with enterprise examples
4. **Analysis Section 2** (300-400 words): Implementation considerations
5. **Analysis Section 3** (300-400 words): Risk/opportunity assessment
6. **Strategic Recommendations** (200-300 words): Actionable guidance
7. **Forward Look** (100-150 words): Future implications

### Word Count Target
- **Minimum**: 1,500 words
- **Target**: 2,000 words
- **Maximum**: 2,500 words

## SEO Guidelines

### Keyword Strategy
- Target enterprise decision-maker searches
- Focus on "strategy", "enterprise", "platform" modifiers
- Include comparison and analysis keywords
- Target featured snippet opportunities

### Meta Description Formula
```
[Action verb] [topic] for [audience]. [Key insight or benefit]. [Unique angle].
```

Example:
> Evaluate multi-cloud strategies for enterprise scale. Analysis of AWS, Azure, and GCP workload placement with TCO considerations and migration pathways.

## Brand Don'ts

- Never use casual or conversational language
- Never make unsubstantiated claims
- Never ignore enterprise-specific concerns (security, compliance, scale)
- Never use click-bait titles or tactics
- Never oversimplify complex strategic decisions
- Never neglect to mention tradeoffs and risks
