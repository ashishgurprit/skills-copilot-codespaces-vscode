## Voice & Tone

### Core Tone
- **Friendly expert**: Like your tech-savvy neighbor
- **Practical**: Actionable advice they can use today
- **Local**: Western Sydney and Hills District focus
- **Accessible**: No jargon, plain language
- **Supportive**: Acknowledge their challenges

### Writing Style
- Use everyday language
- Explain everything, assume no prior knowledge
- Include step-by-step instructions with screenshots
- Reference local examples and businesses
- Provide time and cost estimates

### Vocabulary
**Use**: your business, local customers, get found, grow, attract, website, simple, step-by-step, results, local
**Avoid**: algorithm, technical, complex, enterprise, advanced, sophisticated

## Content Structure

### Ideal Article Structure
1. **Relatable Problem** (75-100 words): "Sound familiar?"
2. **Why It Matters** (100-150 words): Business impact, local context
3. **The Solution** (150-200 words): What to do (overview)
4. **Step-by-Step Guide** (300-400 words): Detailed instructions
5. **Local Tips** (150-200 words): Western Sydney specific advice
6. **What You'll Achieve** (100-150 words): Expected results
7. **Next Steps** (50-100 words): How to get started/get help

### Word Count Target
- **Minimum**: 1,000 words
- **Target**: 1,200 words
- **Maximum**: 1,500 words

## Formatting Guidelines

### For Non-Technical Readers
- Use numbered lists for instructions
- Include screenshots with annotations
- Bold important terms on first use
- Provide time estimates ("This takes about 15 minutes")
- Add "Pro tip" callouts for helpful extras

### Example Callout Format
```markdown
> **Pro Tip**: Save this page and come back to it later.
> You don't have to do everything at once!
```

## Brand Don'ts

- Never use technical jargon without explanation
- Never assume readers have marketing experience
- Never forget the local Western Sydney context
- Never recommend expensive solutions without budget alternatives
- Never be condescending about their current efforts
- Never ignore the time constraints small business owners face
- Never skip the "why" - always explain the benefit
