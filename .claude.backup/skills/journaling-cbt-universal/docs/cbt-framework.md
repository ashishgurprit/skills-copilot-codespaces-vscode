# CBT Framework & Methodology

**Traditional Cognitive Behavioral Therapy concepts for clinical journaling applications**

---

## Overview

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is an evidence-based psychotherapy that helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors.

**Core Principle:** Thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected. By changing thoughts and behaviors, we can improve emotional well-being.

## 2. Thought Record (7-Column Format)

The foundational CBT tool for examining and challenging thoughts.

### Columns

| # | Column | Purpose | Example |
|---|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Situation** | What happened? | "Presentation at work meeting" |
| 2 | **Automatic Thoughts** | What went through your mind? | "I'll mess up and look incompetent" |
| 3 | **Emotions** | What did you feel? (0-100) | Anxiety (85), Fear (70) |
| 4 | **Physical Sensations** | Body reactions | Heart racing, sweaty palms |
| 5 | **Evidence For** | What supports the thought? | "I stumbled on words once before" |
| 6 | **Evidence Against** | What contradicts it? | "Prepared thoroughly, past presentations went well, team is supportive" |
| 7 | **Balanced Thought** | More realistic perspective | "I'm well-prepared. Even if I make a small mistake, it won't define my competence" |

### Post-Balancing

- **Emotions Re-rated:** Anxiety (40), Fear (25)
- **Planned Behavior:** Take deep breaths, focus on content not perfection

## 3. Common Cognitive Distortions

**Definition:** Irrational or exaggerated thought patterns that maintain negative thinking.

### The 10 Most Common Distortions

| Distortion | Description | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **All-or-Nothing Thinking** | Seeing things in black-and-white categories | "If I'm not perfect, I'm a total failure" |
| **Overgeneralization** | Single event = never-ending pattern | "I failed this test. I always fail everything" |
| **Mental Filter** | Focus only on negatives, ignore positives | Remembering only the one criticism in a positive review |
| **Disqualifying the Positive** | Positive experiences "don't count" | "They're just being nice, they don't mean it" |
| **Jumping to Conclusions** | Assuming without evidence | |
| - *Mind Reading* | Assuming you know what others think | "They think I'm boring" |
| - *Fortune Telling* | Predicting negative outcomes | "I know I'll fail the interview" |
| **Magnification/Minimization** | Exaggerating negatives, shrinking positives | Catastrophizing a small mistake |
| **Emotional Reasoning** | "I feel it, therefore it's true" | "I feel anxious, so there must be danger" |
| **Should Statements** | Rigid rules causing guilt/frustration | "I should be able to handle this" |
| **Labeling** | Extreme overgeneralization | "I'm a loser" instead of "I made a mistake" |
| **Personalization** | Blaming yourself for external events | "It's my fault the team lost" |

### Implementation

Prompt users to identify which distortions might be present in their automatic thoughts.

## 5. Behavioral Activation

**Principle:** Depression and anxiety decrease when we engage in valued activities.

### Activity Types

| Category | Purpose | Examples |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Pleasure** | Enjoyment, self-care | Reading, music, nature walk |
| **Mastery** | Accomplishment, competence | Completing a task, learning skill |
| **Social** | Connection, support | Coffee with friend, group activity |
| **Physical** | Movement, energy | Exercise, yoga, stretching |
| **Values-Based** | Meaningful, purposeful | Volunteering, creative project |

### Activity Monitoring Format

| Date/Time | Activity | Type | Mood Before (0-100) | Mood After (0-100) | Notes |
|-----------|----------|------|---------------------|--------------------| ------|
| Mon 9am | Morning walk | Physical | Sad: 60 | Sad: 40 | Fresh air helped |
| Mon 2pm | Called friend | Social | Lonely: 70 | Lonely: 30 | Felt connected |

### Activity Scheduling

Users plan activities in advance:
- **What:** Specific activity
- **When:** Day and time
- **With whom:** Alone or social
- **Obstacles:** Potential barriers
- **Solutions:** How to overcome barriers

## 6. Core Beliefs Work

**Definition:** Deep-seated beliefs about self, others, and the world formed early in life.

### Types of Core Beliefs

**Negative Core Beliefs (Common):**
- **About Self:** "I'm unlovable," "I'm incompetent," "I'm defective"
- **About Others:** "People are untrustworthy," "Others will reject me"
- **About World:** "The world is dangerous," "Life is unfair"

**Positive Core Beliefs (Goals):**
- "I am worthy of love"
- "I am capable"
- "I can handle challenges"

### Downward Arrow Technique

Ask "What does that mean about me?" repeatedly to uncover core belief:

```
Thought: "I shouldn't have made that mistake at work"

"It means I'm not good at my job"
  ‚Üì What does that mean about you?
"It means I'm not capable"
  ‚Üì What does that mean about you?
CORE BELIEF: "I'm inadequate"
```

## 7. Exposure Hierarchy (For Anxiety)

**Principle:** Gradual exposure to feared situations reduces anxiety over time.

### Hierarchy Format

| Step | Situation | Anxiety Rating (0-100) | Status |
|------|-----------|------------------------|--------|
| 1 | Think about making phone call | 20 | ‚úì Completed |
| 2 | Write script for call | 30 | ‚úì Completed |
| 3 | Call to schedule appointment | 50 | In Progress |
| 4 | Call to ask question | 60 | Not Started |
| 5 | Make social phone call | 75 | Not Started |

### Exposure Tracking

After each exposure:
- **Peak Anxiety:** Highest during exposure
- **Ending Anxiety:** Final level
- **Learning:** What did you discover?

## 8. Safety Behaviors

**Definition:** Actions taken to prevent feared outcomes that actually maintain anxiety.

### Examples

| Fear | Safety Behavior | Why It's Problematic |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Social judgment | Avoid eye contact | Prevents learning people are friendly |
| Panic attack | Always carry medication | Prevents learning you can cope without it |
| Rejection | Never share opinions | Prevents authentic connection |

### Tracking

- **Situation:** When do you use safety behaviors?
- **Behavior:** What do you do?
- **Short-term effect:** Does it reduce anxiety?
- **Long-term effect:** Does it maintain the problem?

## 9. Values Clarification

**Why:** Therapy homework is more effective when aligned with personal values.

### Value Domains

1. **Relationships** - Family, friends, romantic
2. **Work/Education** - Career, learning, achievement
3. **Health** - Physical, mental wellness
4. **Leisure** - Hobbies, fun, recreation
5. **Spirituality** - Meaning, purpose, faith
6. **Citizenship** - Community, environment, social causes
7. **Personal Growth** - Character, skills, self-improvement

### Values Exercise

For each domain:
- **Importance:** 0-10 scale
- **Current Satisfaction:** 0-10 scale
- **Gap:** Identify areas needing attention

## 10. Relapse Prevention

### Warning Signs Tracking

Users identify personal early warning signs:
- Mood changes
- Behavior changes (isolation, sleep issues)
- Thought patterns returning
- Physical symptoms

### Coping Strategies Inventory

- **What has worked before?**
- **Who can I reach out to?**
- **What activities help?**
- **What thoughts challenge the spiral?**

## Implementation Guidelines

### For Developers

1. **Thought Records** should be the primary feature
2. **Mood tracking** should show trends over time (graphs)
3. **Cognitive distortions** can be suggested by keyword matching
4. **Activity scheduling** should integrate with calendar
5. **Progress notes** should be shareable with therapist (with consent)

### For Clinicians

- This module supports CBT therapy but doesn't replace therapist guidance
- Encourage regular journaling between sessions
- Review entries during sessions to identify patterns
- Customize homework assignments to patient needs

## Evidence Base

CBT is one of the most extensively researched psychotherapies:
- Effective for depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD, eating disorders
- Benefits typically seen within 12-20 sessions
- Skills-based approach with measurable outcomes
