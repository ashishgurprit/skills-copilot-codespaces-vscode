## Step 1: Research Phase

### Source Requirements

**Primary Sources (Required)**:
- PubMed/MEDLINE for clinical research
- Google Scholar for peer-reviewed articles
- Cochrane Library for systematic reviews
- Top-tier journals: Lancet, BMJ, JAMA, Psychological Bulletin, Journal of Clinical Psychology

**Source Verification**:
- Prefer studies from last 5 years (update older knowledge)
- Minimum 3-5 peer-reviewed sources per article
- Australian studies preferred when available
- Check for AHPRA/APS alignment

### AHPRA Compliance Checklist

Before writing, verify content will:

- [ ] NOT make unsubstantiated therapeutic claims
- [ ] NOT guarantee outcomes or cure rates
- [ ] NOT use testimonials that imply guaranteed results
- [ ] Include appropriate disclaimers
- [ ] Use evidence-based language ("research suggests", "studies indicate")
- [ ] Avoid sensationalism about mental health conditions
- [ ] Respect patient privacy and confidentiality principles
- [ ] Not discourage seeking professional help
- [ ] Include crisis resources where appropriate

### AHPRA-Compliant Language

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "We cure anxiety" | "Evidence-based treatment can help manage anxiety symptoms" |
| "100% success rate" | "Research shows significant improvement in many cases" |
| "Best psychologist" | "Experienced, registered psychologist" |
| "Guaranteed results" | "Treatment tailored to individual needs" |
| "Revolutionary treatment" | "Evidence-based approach" |

## Step 2: Writing Guidelines

### Year 8 Reading Level Rules

**Sentence Structure**:
- Average sentence length: 15-20 words
- Maximum sentence length: 25 words
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice preferred

**Vocabulary**:
- Use common words over technical terms
- Define all clinical terms on first use
- Avoid jargon without explanation
- Use analogies to explain complex concepts

**Paragraph Structure**:
- Maximum 3-4 sentences per paragraph
- Clear topic sentence
- Supporting details
- Transition to next paragraph

**Readability Targets**:
- Flesch Reading Ease: 60-70
- Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: 7-8

### Example Transformations

**Too Complex**:
> "Cognitive behavioral therapy utilizes cognitive restructuring techniques to modify maladaptive thought patterns associated with psychopathology."

**Year 8 Level**:
> "Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) helps you change unhelpful thoughts. A psychologist works with you to spot negative thinking patterns. Then you learn new ways to think about situations. This can help reduce anxiety and depression symptoms."

## Step 4: Keyword Optimization

### Keyword Selection

For each article, identify:

**Long-tail Keyword** (primary focus):
- 3-5 words
- Lower competition
- Higher intent
- Example: "ADHD assessment Bella Vista children"

**Short Keyword** (secondary):
- 1-2 words
- Higher volume
- Broader reach
- Example: "ADHD testing"

### Keyword Placement Requirements

| Location | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Title Tag | Focus keyword within first 60 characters |
| H1 Heading | Focus keyword naturally included |
| H2 or H3 | Secondary keyword in at least one subheading |
| First 100 words | Focus keyword in opening paragraph |
| Body text | Focus keyword 3-5 times naturally |
| Meta description | Focus keyword + call to action |
| Image alt text | Descriptive with focus keyword |
| URL slug | Focus keyword, hyphenated |
